Value investing, at its core, is about buying assets for less than they are worth. Simple in theory, difficult in practice.

## The Margin of Safety

Graham's most important concept is the **margin of safety** — buying at a sufficient discount to intrinsic value so that even if your analysis is wrong, you don't lose money.

> "The margin of safety is always dependent on the price paid."

## Mr. Market

The market is a voting machine in the short run and a weighing machine in the long run. Mr. Market is your business partner who offers to buy or sell every day — sometimes rational, sometimes manic or depressed.

Your job is to take advantage of his irrationality, not be driven by it.

## Key Takeaways

1. Focus on **intrinsic value**, not price
2. Insist on a **margin of safety**
3. Ignore short-term market **noise**
4. Be **patient** — good opportunities are rare
